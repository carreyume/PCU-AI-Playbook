01The Problem

Why This Playbook Exists.

PCU operates across multiple countries, entities, and functions. The problems below are not hypothetical — they are happening now, observed directly within the group.

Work Is Slower Than It Should Be
ESG Reports First drafts take 3–5 days involving 1–2 consultants. The same output can be produced in a single session with the right tool and context loaded.
Client Proposals Proposal cycles run 2–3 days of writing and revision. Teams repeat research that already exists internally across entities.
Audit Reporting Auditors spend up to 2 weeks writing a report after completing fieldwork — time that does not generate revenue. The audit tool prototype targets 1–2 hours.
Monthly Reporting Consolidating group performance reports from multiple entities takes 3–5 days of coordinator time. Much of it is formatting and chasing files.
Knowledge Is Siloed and Duplicated
Duplicate Projects Multiple entities have independently built near-identical AI tools — email-reading agents, logo approval tools, OCR digitisation — using separate third parties, with no awareness of each other.
No Central Register The AI project register exists but is hard to find. Incomplete. Not driving the coordination it was designed for.
Staff Turnover When people leave, their institutional knowledge leaves with them. New staff spend weeks asking colleagues questions that could be self-served.
Cross-Entity Friction Briefing another entity, commissioning work, or tracking man-hours across offices is manual, slow, and error-prone — a coordination tax the group pays every month.
AI Adoption Is Uneven
Tool Confusion Claude Enterprise and ChatGPT Enterprise are both active. Many staff do not know which to use, when, or for what. Some are paying for personal subscriptions unnecessarily.
No Shared Prompts No group prompt library. Every user starts from a blank page. Proven prompts that save hours are not shared.
No Visibility Leadership has no view of which teams are using AI, how much, or what it is producing. Adoption is invisible.
Governance Has Gaps
Unsanctioned Tools Staff are using free and personal AI accounts — Gemini, personal Claude Pro — with no data controls. Client data is at risk.
Agent Liability When an AI agent performs an action under a user's identity — and the outcome is wrong — the logs show the user's name. HR and management are not yet prepared for this.
No Accountability Rule The Code of Conduct does not yet explicitly state that AI output reviewed and submitted by a person is that person's responsibility. This is being addressed.
Speed Is the Real Risk
Competitors Are Moving Sustainability consultancies that operationalise AI in the next 6 months will set client expectations for the next 5 years. Late adoption is not a neutral position.
Seats Already Paid For The Enterprise accounts are active. Every month of underutilisation is a direct cost with no return. The infrastructure is there — the activation is not.
Talent Gap Opening AI-fluent staff are becoming a competitive differentiator. Building that capability now — while the market hasn't priced it in — is the window available.
02 rPCU Responsible AI Usage Policy · v0.1

The Rules.
Before the Tools.

The rPCU Responsible AI Usage Policy governs all AI use across the group — employee or contractor, any function, any entity. Reviewed every 6 months by the ICT Innovation Steering Committee.

⚖️
Fairness
AI must not discriminate on race, gender, age, or any protected characteristic.
🔍
Transparency
AI decisions must be explainable. Users must understand what AI is doing.
🧑‍⚖️
Accountability
The organisation and the individual remain responsible for AI outputs. AI does not absorb liability.
🔒
Data Privacy
GDPR and local data laws apply. No client data into unapproved public tools.
🛡️
Safety
AI systems must be robust and monitored. Vulnerabilities must be identified and mitigated.
👤
Human Oversight
AI enhances human decisions — it does not replace them. Always review critical outputs.
🌱
Sustainability
AI use should reduce energy consumption and support PCU's environmental commitments.
📋
Governance
ICT Innovation Steering Committee oversees all AI deployments. New tools need approval.
✅ Encouraged
  • Summarising lengthy documents or reports
  • Drafting internal communications
  • Prototyping automated workflows
  • Speeding up data analysis
  • Summarising meeting actions and minutes
  • Market analysis and idea generation
  • Language translation of documents
  • Adding ideas to the central AI project register on ShareNet
🚫 Prohibited
  • No client data into public or unapproved AI tools
  • No non-public company information into free tools
  • Do not make critical decisions based solely on AI output without human review
  • Do not assume AI output is always accurate
  • Do not train models on client data without explicit approval
  • New tools must be reviewed by the Global IT Team before use
  • Third-party AI vendors must pass the 3rd Party Cyber Risk Policy
Personal accountability — confirmed by the AI Expert Team: If you use AI, do not review the output, and something goes wrong — that is on you. The AI does not carry the responsibility. The Code of Conduct is being updated to make this explicit. Use AI. Review the output. Submit it as yours.

04When to Use Which

One Decision.
Two Seconds to Make It.

The tools are different. The rule is simple. If you are unsure, default to Claude for anything complex and ChatGPT for anything quick or visual.

Use Claude When —
The Task Is Long, Deep, or Technical
  • Uploading multiple documents for analysis — ESG reports, contracts, proposals
  • Writing or reviewing substantial documents — reports, frameworks, assessments
  • Building or maintaining code, scripts, automation, dashboards
  • Agentic tasks spanning multiple steps requiring judgment across large context
  • Research requiring synthesis across many sources simultaneously
  • Desktop automation via Cowork — Excel, Word, file management (with plan confirmation on)
  • Anything needing the 500K window — full codebases, annual reports, multi-year data
Use ChatGPT When —
The Task Is Fast, Visual, or Already in Microsoft
  • Quick conversational tasks — brainstorming, Q&A, idea generation
  • Image or graphic creation — presentation visuals, concept sketches, social content
  • Working directly in Teams, Word, Excel, or Outlook via Copilot
  • Using a Custom GPT already built for a specific PCU function
  • Multimodal tasks — combining images, PDFs, and text in a single prompt
  • High-volume casual use where the flat-rate seat cost is a factor
  • Staff who prefer a conversational, chat-first interface for everyday tasks

The biggest hidden cost in a multi-country group is not salaries — it is coordination. Emails, handover delays, duplicated work, and man-hours that vanish into inter-entity admin.

✉️
Cross-Entity Communication

Language, tone, and context gaps between offices cause delays and misalignment. Claude drafts, translates, and adapts communications between entities — with full institutional context loaded from the sending entity's Project.

Commission & Task Handover

When work is commissioned across entities, the brief itself is the most common source of delay and rework. Claude standardises the briefing format, auto-generates scopes of work, and tracks obligations in a shared Project accessible to both sides.

11Governance — Built-In Controls

Controls That Come
With the Accounts.

No custom infrastructure needed. These are built-in features — they just need to be activated and owned.

🔐
Identity — SSO + Entra ID

Connect to Microsoft Entra ID once — domain capture automatically provisions every PCU entity email. Users log in with existing Microsoft credentials. No separate password management.

Owner: IT · Time: 1 day
💰
Spend — Admin Limits

Claude Enterprise admin panel lets you set spend limits at organisation or per-user level. ChatGPT Enterprise is flat-rate per seat. Azure Cost Management tracks Claude spend alongside all cloud costs when running via Azure Foundry.

Owner: Finance + IT · Time: 2 hours
📊
Visibility — Compliance API → Power BI

Claude's Compliance and Analytics APIs give programmatic access to usage data and adoption metrics. Pipe into the Power BI instance PCU already uses. Group-level AI utilisation becomes a live report.

Owner: AI Expert Team · Time: 1–2 weeks
📁
Knowledge — Projects + Custom GPTs

One Claude Project or Custom GPT per entity, populated with core documents. Maintained by the AI Champion in each entity. Updated quarterly by the AI Expert Team to keep context current.

Owner: AI Champions · Time: 1 week
📋
Policy — One Page, Practical

Three questions answered: which tool for which task, what data can go in, who to contact if unsure. Based on the rPCU Responsible AI Usage Policy v0.1. Published in Teams. Maintained by the AI Expert Team every 6 months.

Owner: AI Expert Team + HR · Time: 3 days
🔒
Data — No Training on PCU Content

Both Enterprise accounts contractually guarantee PCU's data is not used to train models. Claude on Azure Foundry stays within Azure's boundary — the same as SharePoint and OneDrive. Cowork access to Office 365 is currently restricted pending further governance review.
